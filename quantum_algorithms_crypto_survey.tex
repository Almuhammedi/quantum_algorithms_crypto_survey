\section{Introduction}
Modern digital security relies heavily on public-key cryptographic systems such as RSA and Elliptic Curve Cryptography. These systems derive their security from the computational hardness of mathematical problems such as integer factorisation and the discrete logarithm problem. However, the emergence of large-scale quantum computing introduces significant threats to these cryptographic assumptions, most notably through algorithms such as Shor's Algorithm, which can efficiently solve problems previously considered intractable for classical computers.

In response to these challenges, the field of Post-Quantum Cryptography has developed cryptographic schemes designed to remain secure even in the presence of quantum adversaries. To facilitate global standardisation, the National Institute of Standards and Technology initiated a multi-year evaluation process that resulted in several new standards, including FIPS 203, FIPS 204, FIPS 205, and FIPS 206.

The purpose of this research is to survey recent quantum algorithms capable of breaking classical cryptographic systems and to examine their implications for the security of newly standardised post-quantum cryptographic schemes.
